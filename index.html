About me

I’m a resilient and resourceful web developer and engineer who’s hungry to align continuous learning with positive impact.

I came to this field from 12 years in digital media production, where I employed highly technical skill sets, expert-level communication, methodical organization, and a love for contributing to collaborative efforts and shared visions.

I recently completed a full-stack software engineering bootcamp with General Assembly, where I learned to build full-stack web applications using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Django, Python, Node.js, Express, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, and React.

Being a curious person, I'm drawn to learning how things work (or don't work) so, along with a love for creating useful, uncomplicated, and innovative projects, I am also very interested in testing and debugging.

I’m excited to continue learning and growing as a developer, and I'm eager to contribute!

Specialties

Here are some of the things I do best.

Web Design

I love to create clean, simple, and intuitive user experiences. I'm a big fan of the "less is more" approach, and I'm always looking for ways to make the mundane more exciting.

Web Development

Good design is only part of the battle, making things work (and work better) is the other. I love to build things that are useful, uncomplicated, and innovative.

Graphic Design

As much as a picture or video can leave an impression, so can a logo, a font, or a color scheme. I love to create visual identities that are unique and memorable while guiding the viewer with design principles like proportion, emphasis and hierarchy.

Photography & Videography

After 12 years in digital media production, I've become a highly effective visual storyteller, skilled in the use of just about any camera system and Adobe's Creative Suite.

Marketing Strategy

Being "That Guy"

Okay, so this isn't really a specialty, but I really enjoy being "that guy." The quick learner, the reliable one, the guy who keeps the team in good spirits...working together is about more than the work, am I right?
